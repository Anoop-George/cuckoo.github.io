Saudi job market analysys during covid pandemic.

Recent survey shows that most firms did not cut jobs.

  • Most organizations are likely to increase the salary this year.

  • As 2021 begin most of the job trends are known

  • HR firm Cooper Fitch showed how much the job market was disrupted this year, as companies were forced to delay decisions on hiring new staff and awarding bonuses and pay rises, basic salary ranges and performance goals remained unchanged.

  • Cooper Fitch, said that there is continued high level of uncertainty among businesses, a trend will continue until throughout 2021. Some reports says that, there had been no changes to their recruitment practices in 2021, and that they were still hiring for replacement positions.

  • Most of the business was either busy or had started to pick up. KSA market showed that 79 percent of companies had made no redundancies in 2021, 56 percent said business was either busy and 23 percent said they had implemented a hiring freeze across all roles in 2021. Most of the KSA firms said they would increase salaries in 2021-22, but many were unsure, and an even greater percentage had yet to decide whether to pay bonuses. Respondents’ priorities for the year ahead was to finalize 2021 budgets and make bonus decisions, followed by contingency planning and “manpower planning,” which means hiring new staff and addressing skill gaps.

  • In both the UAE and KSA, technology, advisory and financial services firms performed the strongest amid a challenging years, while real estate and the public sector struggled the most.
